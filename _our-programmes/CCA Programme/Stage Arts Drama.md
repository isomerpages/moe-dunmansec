<h3>Introduction</h3>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/To_place_under_the_INTRODUCTION_heading.jpg">
</div>
<p>StageArts Drama train our members to live in the spirit of performance,
encompassing the following aims:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>We want to be confident in speaking;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We want to express ourselves better;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We want to be able to convince others;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We want to learn to work well with others;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We want to learn what makes a good presentation (theatre/ speech);</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We admire and appreciate good performances;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We are committed and disciplined;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We always want to do the best for our team.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>We believe the spirit of performance encompasses life skills that stay
with members throughout their lives and allow them to apply these skills
in situations within and beyond the school context.</p>
<p>Each StageArts Drama member is known as a Drama StageArtisan.</p>

<h3>Trainings</h3>
<p><strong>We learn:</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Physical Theatre – Control of body movement for acting and confidence</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Voice Projection – What you speak should be heard</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Articulation – What you speak should be clear</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Improvisation – What you do is about creativity and exploration</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Stagecraft – What you do is an artistic creation of the stage/ presentation</p>
</li>
</ul>
